Make Applying to LACity Work Easier

We want you to work for the City of Los Angeles. But the process can seem confusing. Understanding the process can help make it easier to persue a public service career.

There are 3 different types of City Job:

  • Exempt
  • Civil Service
  • Contractor

Regular jobs (also called "civil service" or "non-exempt") are the majority of jobs in the City. And these can initially seem bureaucratic: An applicant takes a test specific to that job, gets a score, and interviews in very structured panel interviews. But if you get the job, there's tremendous job security. (For example, if you're "exempt" and you work for a councilmember, when he loses an election, you're probably out of a job. But that wouldn't be the case with a "regular" job.)

Here's the list of current exams that are open for "regular" jobs at the Government Jobs site.

Exempt Jobs are listed at the Personnel Department Site. Applying for these positions is as simple as submiting a resume.

Contractor Jobs are for IT Contract Professionals within the City. (Think DevOps, Database Adminstrator, Data Scientist among a few examples). The Information Technology Agency maintains a contract with 7 Professional Contract Firms. You have to contract them to apply for the jobs that are typically annual contract jobs. However, the process for getting hired as a contractor is much shorter than getting hired as regular/civil service employee.
